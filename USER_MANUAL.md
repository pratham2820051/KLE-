### 1. What is this System?
This portal is a secure digital register designed for the Rehabilitation and De-addiction Unit at KLE Centenary Charitable Hospital in Belagavi. It replaces old paper logbooks.

It helps you:
- Save patient contact information and medical files.
- Keep track of their family background, addiction history, and past treatments.
- Record notes from counseling sessions.
- Download full reports as PDF files.
- Use built-in calculators (AI models) to predict patient recovery and risk levels.

---

### 2. How to Log In (Access the System)
To enter the system, you must sign in.
1. Open the portal in your web browser.
2. You will see the Home Screen with a welcome message.
3. On the right side, find the Welcome Back login card.
4. Type in your Email ID and Password.
5. Click the Sign In button.

Note on Who can log in:
- Administrator (Admin): The supervisor. The default login is admin@admin.com with the password 123456.
- Counsellors and Nurses (Faculty): The clinical staff. Their accounts are created by the Admin. Once created, they log in using their own email and password.

---

### 3. Understanding the Dashboard
After logging in, you will land on the Dashboard.

- The Left Sidebar Menu:
  - Click the Menu icon (three horizontal lines at the top left) to expand or collapse the sidebar.
  - Dashboard: Shows statistics (number of patients admitted or discharged).
  - Patients: Opens the list of all patients.
  - Manage Faculty (Admin only): Used to add or view staff accounts.
  - Sign Out: Click this at the bottom of the sidebar to log out safely.

- Quick Statistics Cards:
  - Total Patients: Number of all patients ever registered.
  - Admitted: Patients currently staying at the center.
  - Discharged: Patients who completed treatment and went home.

### 4. Searching and Filtering Patients
To find a patient quickly, click Patients in the sidebar.

1. The Search Bar: Type anything you know about the patient in the search box (top right). You can search by:
   - Name (for example: Ramesh)
   - Patient ID number (for example: KLE-0045 or just 45)
   - Phone Number
   - Aadhar Card Number
2. Status Filter Buttons: Click these buttons to filter the list:
   - All: Shows everyone.
   - Admitted: Shows only patients who are currently in the unit.
   - Discharged: Shows only patients who have left.
3. Clear Search: Click the X button in the search box to clear your search and start over.

### 5. How to Add a New Patient (Step-by-Step)
To register a new patient, click the blue Add Patient button on the Patients page. This opens a 6-Step Form. Fill in the details for each step, and click Next to move forward.

#### Step 1: Basic Info (Personal Details)
- Aadhar Number: Enter the 12-digit Aadhar number.
  Note: The system automatically encrypts this number. Even if hackers access the database, they cannot read this number.
- Name, Age, and Gender.
- Address, District, and Taluk.
- Contact Details: Phone number and emergency relative details.
- Socio-demographics: Education level, job type, marital status, monthly income, and religion.
- Referral: Who recommended this rehab center? (Self, Friend, Family, Doctor, etc.)
- Joining Date: The date the patient is admitted.

#### Step 2: Medical and Addiction History
- Addiction Grid (What drug do they use?): Click Add Line to add a drug. Fill in:
  - Drug Type: Alcohol, Nicotine, Cannabis, Stimulants, etc.
  - Drug Name: The specific product (for example: whiskey, beedi, ganja).
  - Age of First Use and Years of Use.
  - Quantity and Frequency: How much and how often did they consume it in the last 30 days?
  - Route: Oral (swallowing), smoking, inhaling, etc.
- Medical Details: Height, Weight, and Blood Sugar level.
- Withdrawal Symptoms: Select what happens when they try to stop (shaking hands, heavy sweating, sleeplessness, fits, vomiting).
- Medical Complications: Check off any past or present illnesses (jaundice, head injury, liver disorders, heart issues, diabetes, depression).

#### Step 3: Family, Work and Legal Status
- Family History: Does anyone else in the family abuse alcohol or drugs? (Father, Mother, Brother, etc.)
- Spouse Details: Wife's name, age, and job.
- Marital Problems: Have they separated from their spouse because of addiction? Is there any family violence (verbal abuse or physical hitting)?
- Work History: At what age did they start working? Have they lost jobs or received warnings because of drinking or drugs?
- Financial Debts: Do they owe money? Enter the debt amount and where they borrowed it (banks, local lenders, friends).
- Legal Troubles: Have they been arrested? Fined for drunken driving? Had accidents under the influence?

#### Step 4: Past Treatment History
- Have they been admitted to a rehabilitation center or hospital before?
- If yes, write down the Hospital Name, Year, and Sober Period (how many months they remained clean after that treatment).
- Select the Reason for Relapse (why they started using again, for example: friends forced them, stress, strong craving).

#### Step 5: Counsellor Section (Narrative Notes)
- Childhood History: Describe their childhood years. Did they experience poverty, parental loss, or family violence before age 15?
- Teenage Behavior Problems: Did they run away, fight, steal, or fail school?
- Education and Hobbies: Years of school, school achievements, extracurricular activities.
- Psychological Analysis: What emotions or stress trigger their addiction?
- Doctor Notes: Write special observations for the consulting medical doctors.
- Follow-Up Logging: Set up the logs for monitoring the patient's progress over time.

#### Step 6: Prediction Models (Calculators)
- This page displays buttons to launch calculations that analyze patient data to help predict their treatment journey.

Warning: Form Redirection
If you skip mandatory fields (like the patient's name or joining date in Step 1) and try to submit the form in Step 5, the portal will prevent the submission, show a warning, and automatically redirect you back to Step 1 while highlighting the empty fields in red.

### 6. How to View, Edit, and Log Follow-ups
Once a patient is registered, you can view and update their profile.

- Viewing a Patient Profile:
  - Find the patient in the table.
  - Click the Blue Eye icon on the right side.
  - This opens a page displaying all their saved records in folders.
- Editing a Patient Profile:
  - Click the Green Pencil icon next to the patient's name.
  - This opens the multi-step form pre-filled with their information.
  - Make the required changes, navigate to the final step, and click the Update button.
- Adding Follow-Up Notes:
  - When a patient returns for a check-up, open their profile in Edit mode.
  - Go to Step 5 (Counsellor Section).
  - Scroll to the bottom to find Follow-Up Notes.
  - Type the date, the patient's current sobriety status (clean or relapsed), and notes on how they are coping. Save changes.

### 7. How to Use the AI Prediction Tools
The portal contains three prediction calculators. You can access them by clicking the buttons on the dashboard or inside Step 6 of the Patient Registration form:

1. Sober Period Prediction:
   - What it does: Estimates how many months the patient is likely to remain clean/sober after leaving rehab.
   - How to use: Fill out the short form (Marital Status, Smoking habit, Motivation factor, Treatment willingness, Blood Sugar level, Relapse count). Click Submit to see the predicted number of months.
2. Risk Level Prediction:
   - What it does: Classifies the patient's risk of relapsing (Low Risk, Medium Risk, High Risk, or Very High Risk).
   - How to use: Answer the questions regarding family support, withdrawal severity, and stressors, then submit.
3. Age of Onset (AAO) Prediction:
   - What it does: Predicts at what age a person is most vulnerable to starting substance abuse based on social, psychological, and family background parameters.

### 8. How to Download Reports and Excel Files
Sharing data with supervisors, doctors, or exporting it for meetings is simple:

- Download a Patient's Complete Case File (PDF):
  - Go to the Patients list.
  - Find the patient and click the Red PDF icon at the end of their row.
  - A formatted PDF file containing all 7 sections of their clinical record will automatically download to your computer.
  - You can print this PDF or send it to doctors.

- Download All Patient Data (Excel Sheet - Admin Only):
  - Log in as an Admin and navigate to the Patients tab.
  - Optional: If you want to download records within a specific timeframe, select the From and To dates in the calendar filters.
  - Click the Download CSV button.
  - An Excel spreadsheet containing all patient profiles, background data, and drug complaints will download instantly.

### 9. Administrator Controls (Managing Staff and Charts)
Administrators have access to special tools to supervise the clinic:

- Adding a Counsellor/Nurse Account:
  - Go to the sidebar and select Counsellor (or Faculty).
  - Click the green Add Counsellor button.
  - Type their Name, Email ID, select their Role (Faculty / Nurse), and assign a Password.
  - Click Save. The staff member can now log in using their credentials.
- Removing a Counsellor Account:
  - Find the staff name in the list, and click the Red Trash Bin icon. Confirm to delete.
- Viewing Age Distribution Charts:
  - The Admin home screen displays a Bar Chart showing how many patients fall into different age ranges (e.g., 18-25 years, 26-32 years, etc.).
  - Hover your mouse pointer over any bar in the chart to see the exact number of patients in that age group.

### 10. Language Settings (English, Kannada, Hindi)
To make the portal easy to use for regional patients and staff, you can change the language:
1. Look at the top-right corner of the screen next to your profile.
2. You will see a Language Selection Dropdown (with options like English, Kannada, Hindi).
3. Select your preferred language.
4. The sidebar menus, table headings, and form labels will instantly translate to the chosen language.

### 11. Troubleshooting and Tips
- System is slow or won't load data: Check if your internet or local hospital network is connected. If offline, the database cannot be queried.
- Cannot save a patient record: Make sure you filled out all mandatory fields in Step 1.
- Cannot log in: Double-check your spelling. Remember that passwords are case-sensitive. If you forgot your password, click Forgot Password on the home screen to send a reset link to your email.
- PDF download doesn't start: Make sure your browser isn't blocking pop-ups. If prompted, select Allow downloads from this website.
