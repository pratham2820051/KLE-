1. What is this System?

This software is a secure, digital register built for the Rehabilitation and De-addiction Unit at KLE Centenary Charitable Hospital in Belagavi. It is designed to replace old paper logbooks that get damaged, lost, or are difficult to search.

The system helps hospital staff, counsellors, and administrators do the following:

  • Save Patient Files: Store names, contact numbers, and background details securely.
  • Track Progress: Log drug addiction profiles, medical issues, family background, and recovery histories.
  • Write Session Notes: Record counseling summaries and plan follow-up check-ups.
  • Get Reports: Download printable PDF summaries of each patient's files.
  • Calculate Recovery (AI): Use simple question-based forms to predict how long a patient will stay sober and check their risk of returning to addiction.

2. How to Log In

To start using the system, you must sign in with your email account.

  1. Open the portal link on your computer or tablet.
  2. You will see the home screen displaying hospital banners.
  3. On the right-side box labeled Welcome Back, type your registered email address and password.
  4. Click the blue Sign in button.
User Roles and Accounts:

• Administrator (Admin): Can create/delete accounts, download global spreadsheets, and view hospital graphs. Default login is admin@admin.com with password 123456.
• Counsellors / Nurses (Faculty): Register patients, write notes, and log follow-ups. Accounts are created manually by the Admin.

3. Using the Sidebar and Dashboard

Once logged in, the screen shows a sidebar menu on the left and statistics cards on the right.

  • Toggle Sidebar Menu: Click the three lines icon at the top-left to hide or show the labels in the sidebar.
  • Quick Statistic Cards:
    • Total Patients: Cumulative patient records saved in the system.
    • Admitted: Patients currently staying in the rehabilitation unit.
    • Discharged: Patients who completed treatment and went home.
  • Log Out: Click the exit door icon at the bottom of the sidebar to sign out safely.

4. Searching and Filtering Patients

To view the list of patients, click Patients in the sidebar. You can search, filter, and print files from this screen.

1

The Search Bar

Type the patient's name, phone number, ID number (for example: KLE-0012), or Aadhar number. The list will update instantly as you type.

2

Status Buttons

Click All, Admitted, or Discharged to filter patients based on their current clinical stay status.

3

Clear Search

Click the small X icon inside the search bar to reset the filter and display the full patient directory.

5. How to Add a New Patient (6 Steps)

To register a patient, click the blue Add Patient button in the top-right corner of the Patients screen. Fill out each step and click Next to proceed.

1
Step 1: Basic Info (Personal Details)

Enter the patient's Name, Age, Gender, Education, Job, Income, Religion, Joining Date, and emergency contact details.
*Note on Privacy:* The patient's Aadhar number is automatically encrypted at the database level so no unauthorized person can read it.

2
Step 2: Medical and Addiction History

Log details of the substances they use. Click Add Line to specify the drug type (e.g., alcohol, beedi, ganja), age of first use, how many years they used it, and route of intake. Enter height, weight, and blood sugar. Check any withdrawal symptoms experienced (e.g., shaking hands, fits, insomnia) and other medical or psychiatric problems.

3
Step 3: Family, Work and Legal Status

Record family details (spouse info, years married). Select if family members have addiction issues. Record work history (reasons for job loss, unemployment). Write down financial details (how much debt they have and who they borrowed from). Note legal complications (arrests, drunken driving accidents).

4
Step 4: Past Treatment History

Check if the patient was admitted to a rehab center in the past. Save the hospital name, the year, and how long they stayed sober after treatment. Record the reason why they relapsed (started using again).

5
Step 5: Counsellor Section (Notes and Logs)

Describe their childhood history (e.g., poverty, parenting issues before age 15) and school behavior problems (stealing, fighting). Explore why they relapse. Write doctor-specific instructions and log initial follow-up schedules. Click Submit to save the patient.

6
Step 6: Prediction Models

Use the portal's automated calculations to analyze recovery and relapse metrics (see Section 7).

Missing Fields Alert:

If you skip mandatory fields (such as the patient's name, age, or date of admission in Step 1) and click submit, the system will prevent saving, display a pop-up warning, and jump back to Step 1 highlighting the blank fields.

6. Viewing, Editing, and Logging Follow-ups

Patient records can be updated at any time (for example, when they return for check-ups or are discharged).

  • View Profile: Click the Blue Eye Icon to view the structured patient profile in clean, readable folders.
  • Edit File: Click the Green Pencil Icon next to the patient's row. Modify any details in the forms. Review the changes, go to Step 5, and click the Update button.
  • Logging Follow-up Visits:
    • Open the patient's record in Edit mode.
    • Go to Step 5 (Counsellor Section).
    • Scroll to the bottom to find the Follow-Up Notes grid.
    • Enter the visit date, choose their recovery status (e.g., Sobriety maintained, Relapsed), write down notes on their physical/mental state, and save the form.

7. How to Use the AI Prediction Tools

The system uses automated calculations (machine learning predictions) to help counsellors plan treatments. Click the prediction buttons on the dashboard or inside Step 6 of the patient form:

Prediction Model What it calculates Required Information to Type
Sober Period Prediction Predicts how many months the patient is likely to remain clean/sober after leaving. Marital status, Smoking habit, Willingness for treatment, Blood Sugar, Relapse count, and Sobriety duration.
Risk Level Prediction Estimates if the patient has a Low, Medium, High, or Very High risk of relapse. Substance type, withdrawal severity, stressors (family issues, debts), and work stability.
Age of Onset (AAO) Prediction Predicts the age range when the individual likely started substance abuse. Childhood experiences, family history of alcoholism, and education background.

8. Downloading Reports and Excel Data

Exporting documents for doctors, clinical reviews, or administration meetings is straightforward.

Individual PDF Report

Find the patient in the list. Click the Red PDF Icon in the actions column. A formatted 7-section report containing all intake details, medical issues, and history will download automatically. Print or email it directly to clinical supervisors.

Download All Data (Excel/CSV)

(Admin only) Go to the Patients page. If you want a specific range, set the "From" and "To" dates in the calendar filters. Click the blue Download CSV button to get a full Excel-compatible spreadsheet of all patient records.

9. Administrator Controls (Manage Staff and Charts)

Administrators have a dashboard featuring extra controls for supervising the unit.

  • Add Counsellor Accounts:
    1. Select Counsellor (or Faculty) from the sidebar.
    2. Click the green Add Counsellor button.
    3. Enter their Name, Email address, select their Role (Faculty or Nurse), and assign a secure password. Click Save.
  • Remove Counsellor Accounts: Click the Red Trash Icon next to the counsellor's name and confirm to remove them.
  • Monitor Age Distribution: The Admin Home screen displays a bar chart representing patient age groups (e.g. 18-25, 26-32, etc.). Move your cursor over any bar on the chart to read the total patient count in that group.

10. Language Settings

To support regional staff and patients, the application has built-in translation options.

  1. Locate the language dropdown in the top-right corner of the topbar menu.
  2. Select either English, Kannada, or Hindi.
  3. The system will instantly translate form labels, buttons, navigation menus, and patient tables to the selected language.

11. Quick Troubleshooting and Help

  • The screen is blank or data is not loading: Make sure your device has a working local network or internet connection. If the database cannot be reached, the list will appear empty.
  • I cannot log in: Check for typing mistakes. Login emails and passwords are case-sensitive. If you have forgotten your password, click Forgot Password on the login screen to receive a reset link.
  • The forms will not save: Go back to Step 1 and check if any fields marked with a red star (or highlighted in red) are left blank.
  • PDF is not opening: Ensure your browser pop-up blocker is turned off, or check your browser's Downloads folder.
